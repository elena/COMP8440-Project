The "Itch"

Linux Conference Australia

History of Linux Conference

  • Conference of Australian Linux Users (CALU): Melbourne in 1999
    (unconference style)
  • Renamed to linux.conf.au in 2001

These are the kinds of people who scratch their own itches and write their own conference software.

  • php (learnt one weekend for the purpose)
  • perl (popular in early 2000s)

New version of the conference management was written in language "du jour"
by whoever was motivated. Up until 2007 ...

Zookeepr: Technical Web Team

There is an annual hackfest. They're a little bit fussy about who participates but not too fussy.

"Hacking team"

Annually (for each LCA)
  • Team of ~8-9
  • Actual core developers: about 5
  • Leader: Zookeepr "Keepr"
  • Under Conference Technical Manager

Zookeepr: Hackfest

"Keeper" hands over to the next team the following year.

Who makes decisions?

What they say is: "Everyone is an adult", we trust that you'll do what you'll say that you'll do and speak up if you have a problem.

'Cathedral' or 'Bazaar'?

It's about in the middle. It's not that exclusive a team, and development seems to be quite "flat" and independent.
